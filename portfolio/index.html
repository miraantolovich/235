About

Mira Antolovich
2nd Year Game Developer at Rochester Insitute of Technology (RIT)

I am a second year Game Design and Developer at Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT). In my free time, I enjoy working with animals at my local animal shelter, playing D&D with my friends, and playing video games. I hope to get a portfolio website out of this class that I can use and link in my resume. I want to create video games that people will enjoy. I want to learn the skills to be able to create beautiful games that will bond people together. To do this, I plan on applying for the Masters program along with a minor in Computer Science. I don't know exactly where I want to end up at the end of my RIT career, but I hope to find out soon.

Projects

Currently, I am working on three major projects. These range from Unity games to creating databases.

Riptide

I am working with a team of seven other people to create Riptide. Riptide is an open-world RPG style game in the ocean where you play as a pirate ship, killing monsters and bosses while upgrading your ship.

Dungeon Kill You

I am working with a team of three other people to create Dungeon Kill You. This is a game that is a local four-player game where one person plays as the dungeon master, trying to kill the dungeon explorers with traps and monsters.

Animal Shelter Database

I am working alone to create a SQL Server Database that is an example of an animal shelter. It handles customers, animals, volunteers/employees, and medical records.

Academic Projects

I worked with a team of three other people to create Captain Bobby's Revenge,a side-scrolling shooter in Visual Studio Monogame. You play as Captain Bobby defending the world from waves of armed-to-the-teeth fish.

Game Jams

Wild MAGIC! - October 2019

I worked with a team of four other people to create Negative Confessions, a narrative game where you are Baby Anubis Jr. trying to figure out whether or not the people you are investigating are telling the truth.

GMTK 2019 - August 2019

I worked with one other person to create One Moment, Please, a turn-based strategy game. You play as a call center employee trying to answer a call before the robots get on the line.

RIT Game Developer's Game Jam - January 2019

I worked with a team of two other people to create Daily Dunce, a mulit-faceted game where you play as a drunk man going about doing daily tasks like feeding a cat and getting groceries (more beer).
